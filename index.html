Method
Tokens are estimated locally in your browser — nothing is sent anywhere. The estimator uses a BPE-style heuristic calibrated to cl100k_base (the tokenizer family used by GPT-4, GPT-4o, and GPT-3.5). Claude uses a different tokenizer, so its estimate here is approximate (typically ±10%).
What the estimator does
It splits text on whitespace and punctuation, then for each chunk: short common words count as 1 token, longer words are split into subword chunks (~4 chars each), digits are grouped in pairs, and each line break, tab, or punctuation mark counts as its own token. The result is usually within 1–3% of the real tokenizer for English prose.
Why your numbers shift by model
Different models use different tokenizers. A 100-word English prompt is roughly 130 GPT-4 tokens, ~130 GPT-4o tokens, and ~120 Claude tokens. Code and non-English text can vary far more.
Rule-of-thumb conversions
• 1 token ≈ 4 characters of English text
• 1 token ≈ ¾ of a word
• 100 tokens ≈ 75 words ≈ 1 short paragraph
• 1,000 tokens ≈ 750 words ≈ 1.5 pages
